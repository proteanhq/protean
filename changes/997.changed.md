Adopted a dual type-safety gate for `src/protean` — `mypy --strict` (bug-catching) plus `pyright` in standard mode (editor/Pylance fidelity, configured in `pyrightconfig.json`). The bulk of the codebase (core, fields, ports, most of server/adapters) is now annotated and passes both checkers; remaining modules stay in the shrinking `[tool.mypy]` quarantine. The pass also fixed several latent runtime bugs it surfaced — a `TypeError` in the SQLAlchemy provider's `_raw`, a `None`-dereference in the Elasticsearch adapter's `_update`, a `KeyError`-contract violation in the `Providers`/`Caches` containers, `None`-safety holes in event-store subscription position handling, an `AttributeError` in `generate docker-compose`'s error path, and unguarded `derive_domain()` results in the IR staleness hooks. See ADR-0018 for the strategy.
