A malformed event-upcaster chain (a duplicate, cyclic, or non-convergent registration) no longer crashes `protean check` with a raw traceback. `@domain.upcaster` now registers through the standard element lifecycle, so the chain is validated by `domain.check()` and a bad chain is reported as a structured error (exit code 1). Upcasters consequently appear in the IR elements index and in `protean ir show`. `domain.init()` still fails fast on a malformed chain, and the `@domain.upcaster(...)` signature is unchanged.
