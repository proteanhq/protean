`protean check` now runs four aggregate & value-object fitness functions, all under the `aggregate_design` category. `CROSS_AGGREGATE_REFERENCE` (warning) flags a `Reference` field pointing at a *different* aggregate's root — the transaction-boundary violation of Vernon's Rule 3 — while leaving the idiomatic child-entity → own-root back-pointer and `HasOne`/`HasMany` associations untouched. `ES_AGGREGATE_NO_EVENTS` (warning) flags an `event_sourced=True` aggregate with no events, which cannot reconstitute its state. `VALUE_OBJECT_MUTABLE_FIELD` (warning) flags a value object with a `List` or `Dict` field, whose mutable state breaks equality-by-value. `AGGREGATE_NO_INVARIANTS` (info) nudges when an aggregate declares no `@invariant.pre`/`@invariant.post` methods. All four honour per-element `suppress_checks` and the `[lint.suppressions]` allow-list.
